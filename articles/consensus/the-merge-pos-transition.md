## Summary

The Merge was Ethereum's transition from Proof-of-Work (PoW) to Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism, which occurred on September 15, 2022. This upgrade replaced energy-intensive mining with a validator-based system, reducing Ethereum's energy consumption by approximately 99.95%.

The transition combined Ethereum's existing execution layer (the original Ethereum Mainnet) with a new consensus layer (the Beacon Chain). Validators now secure the network by staking 32 ETH and are randomly selected to propose and attest to blocks.

The Merge was a crucial step in Ethereum's roadmap, setting the foundation for future upgrades like sharding. It improved security, sustainability, and laid the groundwork for enhanced scalability while maintaining Ethereum's commitment to decentralization.

## Key Concepts

- **Beacon Chain**: The consensus layer that coordinates validators and manages the PoS protocol. Launched in December 2020, it ran in parallel with PoW Mainnet until the Merge.

- **Validators**: Network participants who stake 32 ETH to propose and attest to blocks. They replace miners in securing the network and earn rewards for honest participation.

- **Terminal Total Difficulty (TTD)**: The specific difficulty level at which the PoW chain ended and PoS began, ensuring a smooth transition without requiring a specific block number.

- **Finality**: Under PoS, blocks achieve finality after two epochs (~13 minutes), meaning transactions become irreversible with cryptoeconomic guarantees.

## Personal Notes

The Merge demonstrated that a major blockchain can successfully transition its consensus mechanism without downtime or loss of state. This was unprecedented in the industry and required exceptional coordination between core developers, client teams, validators, and the community.

Key implications for the future:
- Enables further upgrades like proto-danksharding and full sharding
- Changed Ethereum's issuance rate, making ETH potentially deflationary
- Improved network security through cryptoeconomic incentives
- Removed the need for specialized mining hardware, improving decentralization
